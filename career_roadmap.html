Potential Career Paths for Civil Engineering

Civil Engineers can work in a variety of specialized fields. Additional coursework, certifications, and related experience can help support the movement into one of these more specialized civil engineering positions.

Construction Engineers

Construction is one of the nation's largest industries and construction engineers work on a myriad of projects including roads, bridges, or skyscrapers. They plan, manage and supervise these construction projects' materials, labor, and scheduling.

Environmental Engineers

Environmental engineers work to solve a host of environmental issues or problems. They work to create solutions to waste disposal, recycling, water and air pollution control, and similar problems. Environmental engineers use biology, chemistry, soil science and engineering principles to address critical environmental needs.

Geotechnical and Geo-environmental Engineers

These engineers have specialized knowledge about the structure and properties of the earth and how it will impact various construction projects on or below its surface. This helps to determine the safety and feasibility of a construction project. In addition, geotechnical engineers often work to identify potential hazards, such as the likelihood of landslides or seismic activity and then work to mitigate any complicating factors on a building project.

Hydraulic or Water Resources Engineers

Hydraulic engineers specialize in the control and use of water or sewage. These engineers have responsibilities related to storage, regulation, collection, transportation, and control of water or sewage. Analysis of the environment, forces upon the environment, and the behavior of water within the system are key components of a hydraulic engineer's job as they work to build and maintain such systems.

Structural Engineers

Structural engineering focuses on ensuring that load-bearing projects such as bridges, skyscrapers, or tunnels meet strict strength tests. These engineers often work as consultants to architects or design-build contractors. The focus is placed on materials used as they relate to forces placed upon them and how these, in turn, interact with the natural environment.

Transportation Engineers

Transportation engineers can focus on a number of transportation modes, including highway, rail, water, air, and mass transit. These engineers design and plan projects to move people and goods in a safe and efficient manner. They often work with structural or environmental engineers to ensure proper steps are taken to provide strong and environmentally safe solutions to transportation issues.

Urban and Regional Planners

Urban and regional planners work to analyze long-term patterns and data to build or improve new or existing infrastructure to ensure it meets the demands of the community. They must be familiar with local, state, and federal environmental and zoning laws and work to ensure these projects meet these standards. Urban planners often work to provide sustainable development for their communities. They typically have a master's in urban planning, and often hold certification through the American Planning Association.

Utility Engineers

Utility engineers are civil engineers who work specifically to provide utilities such as electricity, gas, and water to their communities. They may design, plan and oversee new projects, or work to maintain existing infrastructure. They may problem-solve to increase the efficiency of these systems or to work to identify the causes for any issues or failures within the systems.

Architectural and Engineering Managers

Civil engineers who desire to work overseeing projects often become architectural and engineering managers. These individuals oversee all aspects of a project and manage teams of engineers working on the project. They make final decisions about what kinds of materials and staffing are required and they take responsibility for ensuring projects are done on time and on budget. Engineering managers confer with clients and upper management to ensure good communication about the project and they typically hold a Master's in Engineering Management or a Master's in Business Administration.

Potential Career Paths for Electrical Power Engineering

Power engineering is the field of electrical engineering and it deals with the electrical energy generation, the transmission of energy, distribution, and utilization of energy. In power engineering, the engineers are responsible for designing, controlling, and monitoring the electrical system.

Service Maintenance Engineer

A service maintenance engineer is responsible to provide service to the device or equipment on a scheduled time, it may monthly bases.

Power Electronics Engineer

The responsibility of a power electronic engineer is to design a power electronic circuit and implement it.

Safety Officer

The role of the safety officer is to implement safety regulations in the fields like power plants, substations, transmission, and distribution lines.

Power Technician

The role of the power technician is to monitor and control all the devices and equipment which are found in power plants, substations, and lines.

Electrical Engineer

The role of the electrical engineer in the power_engineering field is to design circuits and equipment for plants, substations, etc.

Controls engineer

A controls engineer is responsible for developing equipment that is efficient in creating products. They also plan for maintenance and implement processes that reveal problems with their systems. Controls engineers research solutions, test their design and produce efficacy reports that show them how effective their system is.

Test engineer

A test engineer is responsible for conducting tests on electrical systems. They speak with stakeholders to better understand business needs, formulate test plans and analyze usage cases. Test engineers fix the problems they find or offer guidance in implementing solutions.

Electronics engineer

An electronics engineer is responsible for designing, developing and implementing electronics equipment like GPS devices, kitchen appliances and electric motors. They research designs, test components of their design and meet specifications for their work.

Systems engineer

A systems engineer is responsible for making sure certain systems they're in charge of are fully functional and optimized. They may install new software and electronics, monitor its usage and update systems as needed. Systems engineers may form a feedback process to identify any issues and offer solutions to stakeholders.

Potential Career Paths for Information Technology Engineering

If you're an aspiring software engineer, you can expect regular opportunities to progress your career. As you learn technical skills and gain experience, new doors will open. Youâ€™ll have the choice to continue working with code, or to use your understanding of the software development lifecycle to lead teams.

Front-End Engineers

A front-end engineer focuses on building the user interface for a website or application. They're responsible for the look and feel of a website and handle anything a user can interact with.

Back-End Engineers

These professionals work on the server-side of web applications. This includes web services, database design, and integrating data feeds. Shopping cart functionality or a secure payment system are examples of back-end development.

Full-Stack Engineer

If you combine front-end and back-end development, you get full-stack development. Full-stack engineers have a wide range of skills in both user interface design, and technical database configuration.

Mobile App Developer

Mobile engineers build software for smartphones, tablets, and other mobile devices. They need to have a solid understanding of Android and iOS, build responsive apps that work on a range of different devices, and consider technical limitations like memory and processing power.

Graphics Engineer

These engineers use 2D and 3D digital platforms for gaming and video production. In the early days, graphics engineers were required to have expertise in math and computer science. But more recently, open-source frameworks like Unity and OpenGL handle most of the heavy lifting.

Game Engineer

The video game industry is big business and game designers are highly sought-after. The role typically involves modeling physics, 2D and 3D graphics, and game mechanics.

Data Engineer

Software engineers that specialize in big data are called data engineers. They're responsible for storing, organizing, managing, and analyzing information. Data engineers find useful insights from data to help make informed business decisions.

DevOps Engineer

DevOps comes from development and operations. A DevOps engineer is someone who facilitates, oversees, and expedites the process of code release or deployment of applications.

Security Engineer

As the name suggests, these engineers design solutions to safeguard software or networks from hackers or cyber threats.

Potential Career Paths for Mechanical Engineering

A mechanical engineering degree can provide many fulfilling opportunities for students. Because of the degree and technical knowledge requirements, there is often high demand for well-educated engineers. Their skills also lend them to work in many sectors and industries like manufacturing, automobile, biotechnology, agriculture, national defence and nanotechnology.

Automotive engineer

With a job as an automotive engineer, graduates can design and develop the mechanical components and machines related to cars, trains and other automated vehicles. Their job tasks can include research and development, design and production.

Maintenance engineer

Maintenance engineers perform diagnostic checks of machines and equipment to ensure their functionality. Maintenance engineers might work in large plants and work to maintain and large machines like heating and cooling systems, production equipment and engines that are capable of continuous operation.

Research and development supervisor

Research and development supervisors oversee processes related to designing new systems. Depending on the industry, this could mean improving current technology, testing customer reactions to prototypes or researching new advances in the field.

Construction engineer

Construction engineers use their mechanical knowledge on construction projects. They might service and maintain the machines a group is using for a project, advise on the installation of heavy equipment or help devise system optimisation efforts that can aid in the construction project.

Aerospace Engineer

Mechanical engineers can work alongside aerospace engineers in this field. Here, they help maintain and manufacture aircraft. Mechanical engineers' technical knowledge can help ensure designs are compatible with airport needs and other production aspects.

Control and Instrumentation Engineer

Also called a C&I engineer, these mechanical engineers design, develop, install, manage and maintain the equipment that monitors and controls other machines and systems. Engineers make sure all these processes are running as efficiently, safely and reliably as possible.

Nuclear Engineer

A nuclear engineer offers technical support to nuclear power stations. In their role, they may design, build, operate or disassemble machinery used with nuclear power. Specific roles for mechanical engineers in this field include reactor operators, process engineers, quality engineers, C&I engineers and managers.

Mechanical Engineer

A mechanical engineer can be a generic job title within many industries, but the general responsibilities of companies hiring for this position include design, development, maintenance and management. A company may hire a mechanical engineer to test or install new machinery.

Manufacturing engineer

Manufacturing engineers use their degrees to aid them in the manufacturing industry. They focus on the operation and design of integrated systems.
